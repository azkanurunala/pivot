# PRD: Pivot

**Product Name:** Pivot  
**Subtitle:** One swipe. Infinite depth. Master the angle.  
**Version:** 1.0 MVP  
**Target Release:** Week 5  
**Platform:** iOS 15+, offline-first  
**Monetization:** $4.99 premium (100+ handcrafted levels), optional cosmetics ($0.99 trajectory visualizers, ball skins)  

---

## 1. North Star Statement

Players swipe to set launch angle, then watch physics unfold—mastering trajectory, momentum, and bounce angles through a single gesture that hides deep strategic thinking, all offline, zero setup.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

- **Player Pain:** Physics puzzle games (Cut The Rope, Angry Birds) require multiple gesture types (draw, tap, hold). One-gesture games (Flappy Bird, Badland) feel shallow after mastery. No game owns "one gesture = infinite strategic depth."
- **Market Gap:** Angle-based physics games exist (Okay?) but position as "musical bouncing" not "trajectory mastery." Puzzle players want depth, not just pretty bounces.
- **WTP Signal:** Physics puzzle game players pay $4.99-$9.99 for premium. Arcade/skill games monetize via cosmetics. Hybrid model proven.

## 3. Core Mechanic

### 3.1 — The Moneyshot (10 Seconds)

**Screen: Level View**
```
Goal: Hit all ● targets

      ●
      
      
      ●
    ╱─────────┐
   ○ (ball)   │ (wall, angled)
                
[Swipe to set angle]
```

**Action:** Swipe bottom-left to top-right → sets launch angle (0-180°) → release swipe → ball launches → physics unfold → ball bounces off walls → targets hit or miss → level complete or retry.

**Input:** Single gesture (swipe), angle only. Velocity auto-calculated based on target distance.

---

### 3.2 — Physics Depth (Hidden)

**Variables hidden from player (but learned through play):**
- **Angle:** 0-180°, controls trajectory
- **Velocity:** Auto-scaled per level (reaching target optimally)
- **Bounce angle:** Realistic reflection (angle in = angle out + friction loss)
- **Friction:** Decreases velocity per bounce (teaches arc planning)
- **Momentum:** Ball curves path based on walls hit
- **Gravity:** Slight downward pull (affects arc mid-flight)

**Strategic thinking emerges:**
- "If I hit angle 47°, I'll bounce off north wall at 133°, miss target"
- "I need 62° to ricochet correctly"
- "Fewer bounces = cleaner path"

**No UI showing these variables.** Player learns by repeated play + intuition building.

---

### 3.3 — Level Progression (Difficulty Scaling)

**Levels 1-5:** Single target, wide angle range works
- Teach mechanic, build confidence
- Multiple solutions per level

**Levels 6-15:** 2-3 targets, tighter angles needed
- Precision introduced
- Bounce sequences matter

**Levels 16-30:** 4+ targets, complex bounces required
- One solution per level (or very few)
- Mastery phase: player must understand physics

**Levels 31-50:** Expert puzzles
- Extreme angles, tiny target zones
- Bounces chain (5+ bounces)
- "Aha!" moments when solution clicks

**Levels 50+:** Endless/infinite mode
- Procedurally generated
- Score = bounces minimized + targets hit

## 12. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Learning curve:** Angle-only input feels limiting to newcomers | Medium | Levels 1-5 teach with wide angle ranges. Cosmetic trajectory guide helps stuck players ($0.99, optional). Tutorial clear. |
| **Physics bugs:** Collision misalignment breaks trust | High | Use proven physics library (Rapier). Test on 100+ devices. Extensive QA on bounce angles. |
| **Retention:** Plateau after Level 30 (finite content) | Medium | Infinite mode (procedural levels) extends engagement. Daily Challenge creates daily ritual. Optional level editor (post-MVP). |
| **Monetization:** Low cosmetic conversion (puzzle players utilitarian) | Low | Trajectory guide is legitimately useful (not cosmetic-only), increases conversion. Seasonal FOMO tactics. |
| **Difficulty spike:** Level progression too hard around Level 20 | High | Playtesting to smooth curve. Optional hint system (reveal solution angle for cosmetic cost, educational). |

## 15. Definitions

**Angle:** Player input, 0-180° representing launch trajectory.

**Velocity:** Auto-calculated from level (enough to reach target optimally, prevents brute-force guessing).

**Bounce angle:** Realistic reflection (angle in ≈ angle out, minus friction loss).

**Friction:** Per-bounce velocity decrease (teaches planning: fewer bounces = more efficient).

**Mastery:** Visual/intuitive understanding of physics without conscious calculation.

**Low floor, high ceiling:** Beginners can play (wide angle ranges work), experts perfect angles (0.5° precision matters).
